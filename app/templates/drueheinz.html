The Drue Heinz Literature Lectures Poster

To further explore the concept of informational hierarchy in print posters, each student was given a white piece of tabloid paper with black, identically formatted text, and through a series of iterations, tasked with creating a poster advertisement for their assigned event. My event was The Drue Heinz Lecture Series: a literature lecture series run through the Carnegie Music Hall.

The poster is inkjet printed on tabloid paper, and the first image on the left is the final iteration. Below it are some examples of the process I went through to arrive at that final copy. If you're interested in the full process PDF, please click here.
